## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">1. The Opening of the New Testament</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:1</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham.”</span>

The New Testament begins with a genealogy. This is highly significant.  
In the Old Testament we find “the book of the generations of Adam” (Genesis 5:1).  
Here we find “the book of the generations of Jesus Christ.”

These are two different races — one human and the other divine.  
Everyone is originally born into the book of the generations of Adam.  
But some, by repentance and faith, are transferred into the book of the generations of Jesus Christ.

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">2. Two Genealogies of Jesus</span>

### Matthew’s Genealogy (Joseph – Legal Line)
- Traces **Joseph**, the husband of Mary (Matthew 1:16).
- Follows the **royal line** of David through Solomon and the kings of Judah.
- Proves that Jesus is the **legal heir** to the throne of David.
- If there had been a reigning king in Israel at that time, Joseph would have been entitled to the throne, and after him his legal son Jesus.

### Luke’s Genealogy (Mary – Physical Line)
- Traces the line of **Mary**.
- Follows David through his son **Nathan** (Luke 3:31).
- Proves that Jesus is physically “of the seed of David according to the flesh” (Romans 1:3).

**From Adam to David** the two genealogies are the same.  
**At David** they branch into two trees:  
- Joseph through Solomon  
- Mary through Nathan  

Thus Jesus is both the **legal** and **physical** Son of David — fully qualified as Messiah and King.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">3. Why Matthew Begins with Abraham</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:2</span>

Matthew wrote primarily for a **Jewish audience**, so he begins with Abraham, the father of the Jewish nation.  
Luke wrote for the **Gentile world**, so he begins with Adam.

This shows that Jesus is the promised Saviour for both Jews and Gentiles.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">4. The Four Women in the Genealogy</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:3–6</span>

Matthew unusually includes four women — something almost never done in Jewish genealogies. Each had a background of shame, scandal, or outsider status:

### 1. Tamar (v.3) – Genesis 38
Judah’s daughter-in-law. She disguised herself as a prostitute and conceived Perez through her own father-in-law. A story of deception and immorality.

### 2. Rahab (v.5) – Joshua 2 & 6
The prostitute of Jericho who hid the Israelite spies and later married Salmon. A Canaanite woman with a publicly immoral past.

### 3. Ruth (v.5) – Book of Ruth & Genesis 19
A Moabite Gentile. The Moabites originated from the incestuous relationship between Lot and his daughter. Under the Law a Moabite was excluded from the assembly of the Lord.

### 4. Bathsheba (v.6) – 2 Samuel 11
Called simply “the wife of Uriah.” David committed adultery with her and murdered her husband. A story of royal lust, adultery, and murder.

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">5. Theological Significance</span>

Jesus, the only Person who ever chose the family, place, and time of His birth, deliberately chose a line filled with immorality and shame.

He did this because:
- He came **to save sinners**, not the self-righteous (Matthew 9:13).
- He came for the least and the lowest.
- He wanted to show that no background is too shameful for the grace of God.
- He humbled Himself to the lowest place so that He could lift the lowest people.

This should shatter every form of pride in family background, caste, status, or heritage.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">6. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- There are two books of generations. We are all born into Adam’s book, but by repentance and faith we can be transferred into Christ’s book.
- Jesus is both the legal and physical Son of David — fully qualified as Messiah and King.
- God can take the most broken stories and weave them into His redemptive plan.
- Anyone who still feels superior to others because of their background can never be a true disciple of Jesus Christ.
- True discipleship requires radical humility.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">7. Personal Application</span>

- Am I still proud of my family line, caste, education, or social standing?
- Do I look down on people with a “shameful” past or background?
- Have I truly transferred from the book of Adam into the book of Jesus Christ through repentance and faith?
- Am I willing to follow Jesus in humility to the lowest place?
