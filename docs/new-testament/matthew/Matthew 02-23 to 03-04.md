## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">1. “He Shall Be Called a Nazarene”</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:23</span>

Joseph and Mary settled in Nazareth so that what was spoken through the prophets might be fulfilled:  
> <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">“He shall be called a Nazarene.”</span>

This is a remarkable fulfillment. The quotation is linked to **Isaiah 11:1**, where a “shoot” or “branch” (*netzer* in Hebrew) will spring from the stem of Jesse.  

The Hebrew word *netzer* is connected with the name Nazareth.  

<span style="background-color: #fef3c7; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>Important Insight</strong><br>
There is more in Scripture than meets the eye. Many fulfillments are hidden and can only be seen by the revelation of the Holy Spirit. Our clever human minds alone cannot grasp the full depth of God’s Word.
</span>

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">2. Scripture Is Fulfilled Through Both Obedience and Disobedience</span>

In the first two chapters of Matthew we see a repeated phrase:  
**“that the Scripture might be fulfilled”**  
(Matthew 1:22; 2:15; 2:17; 2:23).

- Scripture is fulfilled when people **obey** God (e.g., Joseph).  
- Scripture is also fulfilled when people **disobey** God (e.g., Herod killing the children in Bethlehem).

<span style="background-color: #ecfdf5; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #10b981; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>Great Comfort</strong><br>
The sovereignty of God is so great that even the anger and evil actions of godless people only end up fulfilling God’s Word. Whatever people do will ultimately work for the good of those who love God.
</span>

Even Archelaus reigning in Judea (which made Joseph afraid to settle there) became the means by which God led the family to Nazareth — again fulfilling Scripture.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">3. John the Baptist – A Man Sent from God</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 3:1–3</span>

John the Baptist came preaching in the **wilderness of Judea**, saying:  
> <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">“Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand.”</span>

This was a fulfillment of Isaiah 40:3 — “The voice of one crying in the wilderness…”

**Significant Observation**:  
True men of God do not advertise themselves.  
- Elijah and Elisha did not advertise themselves.  
- John the Baptist did not begin his ministry in the streets of Jerusalem.  
- He went to an uninhabited wilderness.

If John had gone by human reason, he would have started in Jerusalem. Instead, he followed the leading of God.

<span style="background-color: #f0f9ff; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #3b82f6; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>Luke 3:1–2</strong> shows this beautifully:<br>
All the great secular rulers (Tiberius Caesar, Pontius Pilate, Herod, Philip, Lysanias) and the religious big shots (Annas and Caiaphas) are listed — yet the Word of God <strong>bypassed all of them</strong> and came to an unknown, unordained man named John in the wilderness.
</span>

Even today, the Word of God often bypasses the big names in both the secular and religious world and comes to simple men who are anointed by the Holy Spirit.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">4. God Creates the Audience</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 3:5</span>

Although John was preaching in the wilderness, “Jerusalem was going out to him, and all Judea and all the district around the Jordan.”

When God sends a man, **God creates the audience**.  

We do not have to advertise ourselves, promote ourselves, or try to create a following. Those who constantly advertise themselves are usually not true men of God. A true man of God never needs to promote himself.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">5. The Message of Repentance</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 3:2</span>

John’s central message was:  
> <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">“Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand.”</span>

Repentance is the main message of God’s Word throughout Scripture:
- All the Old Testament prophets preached it  
- John the Baptist preached it  
- Jesus preached it  
- The apostles preached it from the day of Pentecost  
- Jesus still calls the churches to repent in Revelation 2–3  

Tragically, this is the message that is largely missing in much of today’s preaching.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">6. The Kingdom of Heaven vs Earthly Kingdom</span>

The Jews had spent their whole lives looking for an **earthly kingdom** with earthly blessings — overflowing barns, physical wealth, many children, physical healing.

John announced a completely different kingdom — the **kingdom of heaven**.

<span style="background-color: #fef3c7; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>New Covenant Reality</strong><br>
The New Covenant does not lead us to earthly prosperity and physical blessings as the main focus. It leads us to:<br>
• Spiritual prosperity<br>
• Spiritual children<br>
• Spiritual healing (salvation from sin)<br>
• The kingdom of God rather than the kingdom of self
</span>

True repentance means turning around — from seeking earthly things to seeking heavenly things, from the kingdom of self to the kingdom of God.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">7. John’s Simple Lifestyle</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 3:4</span>

John wore a garment of camel’s hair with a leather belt and ate locusts and wild honey.  

He lived a simple life.  

This does not mean every servant of God must be an ascetic. Jesus Himself was not an ascetic — He wore a seamless robe and ate normally. But His life was also marked by simplicity.

**Characteristics of a true man of God seen in John**:
- Simplicity of life  
- Not seeking honour from men  
- Proclaiming the truth even if it means being alone in the wilderness  
- Seeking to lead people to God rather than to himself

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">8. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- It is always safer to go by what God reveals than by our own reason.
- Scripture is fulfilled both through the obedience of the godly and the disobedience of the ungodly.
- True men of God do not advertise themselves.
- When God sends a man, He also creates the audience.
- The central message of God’s Word is still “Repent.”
- The New Covenant is about a heavenly kingdom, not primarily earthly blessings.
- A simple lifestyle and freedom from the love of honour are marks of a true servant of God.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">9. Personal Application</span>

- Do I still lean on human reason, or do I live by the revealed Word of God?
- Am I seeking an earthly kingdom (comfort, success, recognition) or the kingdom of heaven?
- Is the message of repentance central in my own life and in what I share with others?
- Do I feel the need to advertise or promote myself, or do I trust God to create the audience?
- Is my lifestyle marked by simplicity and a desire to lead people to God rather than to myself?
