### <span style="color: #805ad5; font-weight: bold;">Bible Text (NASB)</span>

> <span style="color: #2c7a7b;">**Matthew 2:1–11**</span>  
> <span style="color: #4a5568;">
> 1 Now after Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea in the days of Herod the king, magi from the east arrived in Jerusalem, saying,  
> 2 “Where is He who has been born King of the Jews? For we saw His star in the east and have come to worship Him.”  
> 3 When Herod the king heard this, he was troubled, and all Jerusalem with him.  
> 4 Gathering together all the chief priests and scribes of the people, he inquired of them where the Messiah was to be born.

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">1. The Magi from the East</span>
<img src="https://blog.cph.org/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/CPH_blog/Study/2023/epiphany-wise-men.jpg?width=2000&height=1122&name=epiphany-wise-men.jpg"  width="500">

<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 2:1–2</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Now after Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea in the days of Herod the king, magi from the east arrived in Jerusalem, saying, ‘Where is He who has been born King of the Jews? For we saw His star in the east and have come to worship Him.’”</span>

Wise men (Magi) from a far country (possibly Persia) saw a special star and travelled hundreds of miles to find the newborn King.  

They had **no written Scriptures**, yet God gave them revelation.  
Meanwhile, the chief priests and Bible scholars in Jerusalem, who had the Scriptures, knew nothing about Jesus’ birth.

**Key Lesson**:  
God is no respecter of persons. He reveals Himself to those who seek Him, whether or not they have the written Word. Having the Bible and Bible schools is no guarantee of knowing Jesus.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">2. The Mistake of the Wise Men</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 2:1–8</span>

The star had led them all the way. But when they reached Jerusalem, they stopped following the star and used their **reason**. They thought:  
“The King of the Jews must be born in the capital city, in the palace.”

So they went to King Herod instead of continuing to follow the star.  
This was their first mistake.

**Spiritual Application**:  
The star is a picture of the Word of God (2 Peter 1:19).  
We can follow God’s Word up to a point, then lean on our own understanding (Proverbs 3:5) and go astray.  

The things of the Spirit are foolishness to the natural mind. Jesus was not born in a palace but in a stable.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">3. Herod and the Religious System</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 2:3–8</span>

When Herod heard about a newborn King, he was troubled, and all Jerusalem with him.  

This is a picture of an old religious system that feels threatened by new revelation from God.  
People who have built their own religious or financial “kingdoms” are disturbed when the true King is revealed.

Herod pretended to want to worship Jesus, but his real intention was to kill Him.  
Many today also pretend interest in the gospel while refusing to give up their private kingdoms.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">4. Knowledge Without Revelation</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 2:4–6</span>

Herod asked the chief priests and scribes where the Messiah was to be born.  
They immediately answered from Scripture (Micah 5:2): “In Bethlehem of Judea.”

They had accurate **academic knowledge** of the Bible, but no revelation.  
They could quote the verse, yet they never went to Bethlehem to seek the Child.

**Warning**:  
Years of Bible study and the ability to answer questions promptly are useless without the revelation of the Holy Spirit.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">5. The Star Leads Them to Jesus</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 2:9–11</span>

When the Magi left Herod and looked up again, the star reappeared and led them straight to the house where the Child was.

They rejoiced with **exceeding great joy**.  
They fell down and worshipped the Child (not Mary).  
They presented gifts:
- **Gold** – for a King  
- **Frankincense** – for a Priest  
- **Myrrh** – for a Suffering Servant  

The Word of God, when followed, always leads us to **Jesus Himself**, not merely to doctrines.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">6. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- God reveals Himself to those who seek Him, even if they have no written Scriptures.
- Having the Bible and Bible knowledge is no guarantee of knowing Jesus.
- Never stop following the Word of God and lean on your own reason.
- Religious systems and personal kingdoms feel threatened by the true King.
- Academic knowledge without the Holy Spirit’s revelation is empty.
- The Word of God always leads us to Jesus Christ Himself.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">7. Personal Application</span>

- Am I still following the “star” (God’s Word), or have I started leaning on my own reason?
- Do I have only academic knowledge of Scripture, or do I have living revelation of Jesus?
- Am I willing to leave my “Jerusalem” (comfort, reputation, religious systems) to find the real Jesus?
- When I meet Jesus, do I worship Him alone, or do I give honour to other things/people?
