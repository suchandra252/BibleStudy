## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">1. The Birth of Jesus Christ</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:18</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Now the birth of Jesus Christ was as follows: when His mother Mary had been betrothed to Joseph, before they came together she was found to be with child by the Holy Spirit.”</span>

Mary and Joseph were betrothed (a binding engagement under Jewish custom). Before they came together as husband and wife, Mary was found to be pregnant by the Holy Spirit. This was completely outside human possibility.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">2. Joseph – A Righteous Man</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:19</span>

Joseph is described as a **righteous man**. Even though he was fully convinced in his mind that Mary had been unfaithful, he refused to disgrace her publicly. He planned to divorce her quietly.

This shows the true nature of New Testament righteousness — it protects others rather than exposing and shaming them.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">3. Joseph Considered and Prayed</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:20</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“But when he had considered this, behold, an angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream…”</span>

Joseph did not act impulsively. He “considered this” — he thought carefully and almost certainly prayed. Because he waited on God instead of reacting in the flesh, the Lord was able to speak to him.

**Lesson**: “He who believes will not make haste.” A righteous man does not act on the spur of the moment.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">4. The Angel’s Message</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:20–21</span>

The angel said:

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Joseph, son of David, do not be afraid to take Mary as your wife; for the Child who has been conceived in her is of the Holy Spirit. She will bear a Son; and you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins.”</span>

Two important truths:
- The Child is of the Holy Spirit.
- His name is **Jesus** (Yahweh saves) because His mission is to save His people from their **sins**.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">5. Prophecy Fulfilled – Emmanuel</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:22–23</span>

Matthew connects this to Isaiah’s prophecy:

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Behold, the virgin shall be with child and shall bear a Son, and they shall call His name Emmanuel,” which means “God with us.”</span>  
> (Isaiah 7:14)

- **Jesus** = Yahweh saves  
- **Emmanuel** = God with us  

The eternal God has entered human history.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">6. Joseph’s Immediate Obedience</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:24–25</span>

When Joseph woke up, he obeyed without delay:
- He took Mary as his wife.
- He kept her a virgin until after the birth of Jesus.
- He named the Child Jesus, exactly as commanded.

Joseph set aside his own reasoning and reputation and simply obeyed God in faith. This is a beautiful example of the **obedience of faith**.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">7. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- True righteousness protects the reputation of others.
- Do not act impulsively — take time to consider and pray.
- God speaks to those who wait on Him.
- The work of the Holy Spirit often brings misunderstanding and reproach at first.
- Obedience to God’s Word must override our own reasoning and reputation.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">8. Personal Application</span>

- When I suspect someone has done wrong, do I protect their reputation or expose them?
- Do I react emotionally, or do I take time to think and pray?
- Am I willing to obey God even when it costs me my reputation?
- Do I understand that the genuine work of the Holy Spirit may bring misunderstanding rather than praise?

---

### <span style="color: #805ad5; font-weight: bold;">Prayer</span>

Lord Jesus, thank You for the example of Joseph. Make me like him — careful with the reputation of others, quick to pray, and ready to obey You completely. Deliver me from pride, suspicion, and impulsive reactions. Help me walk in the gentle righteousness of the New Covenant. Amen.
