# <span style="color: #2b6cb0; font-weight: bold;">Joseph – A Righteous Man</span>
<span style="color: #4a5568;">Zac Poonen – Verse-by-Verse Study (Matthew 1:18–25)</span>

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">1. The Setting</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:18–19</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Now the birth of Jesus Christ was as follows: when His mother Mary had been betrothed to Joseph, before they came together she was found to be with child by the Holy Spirit. And Joseph her husband, being a righteous man and not wanting to disgrace her, planned to send her away secretly.”</span>

Joseph and Mary were betrothed. Under Jewish custom, betrothal was a binding commitment almost equal to marriage. Before they lived together as husband and wife, Mary was found to be pregnant.

Joseph was fully convinced in his own mind that Mary had been unfaithful. Yet the Scripture describes him as **a righteous man**.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">2. How Joseph’s Righteousness Was Manifested</span>

Joseph’s righteousness did not express itself in demanding justice or public exposure. Instead:

- He refused to disgrace Mary publicly.
- He did not broadcast the matter all over Nazareth.
- He planned to divorce her quietly so that she would not be put to shame.

This is the first mention of **righteousness** in the New Testament. It shows us the true character of New Covenant righteousness:

> A righteous man does not seek to expose and shame others, even when he believes they are guilty.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">3. He Did Not Act Impulsively</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:20</span>

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“But when he had considered this, behold, an angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream…”</span>

Joseph “considered this.” He did not react in the heat of emotion. He took time to think and, almost certainly, to pray.

The Scripture says, “He who believes will not make haste” (Isaiah 28:16).  
A righteous man does not act on the spur of the moment. He waits, considers the effect on the other person, and seeks God.

Because Joseph was willing to pause and pray, God was able to speak to him.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">4. God’s Revelation and Joseph’s Obedience</span>
<span style="color: #718096;">Matthew 1:20–25</span>

The angel said to him:

> <span style="color: #e53e3e; font-weight: bold;">“Joseph, son of David, do not be afraid to take Mary as your wife; for the Child who has been conceived in her is of the Holy Spirit. She will bear a Son; and you shall call His name Jesus, for He will save His people from their sins.”</span>

Joseph awoke and immediately obeyed.  
He set aside his own reasoning and suspicions.  
He took Mary as his wife.  
He kept her a virgin until the birth of Jesus.  
He named the Child Jesus exactly as commanded.

This was the **obedience of faith** — not the obedience of reason. Reason told him Mary had been immoral. Faith told him that what God had said must be true, even though he could not understand it.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">5. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- True New Testament righteousness protects the reputation of others rather than exposing them.
- A righteous man does not act impulsively. He considers, thinks, and prays before taking serious decisions.
- God speaks to those who are willing to wait on Him instead of reacting in the flesh.
- Obedience to God often requires setting aside our own reasoning and reputation.
- The first work of the Holy Spirit in the New Testament (the conception of Jesus) brought misunderstanding and reproach — not popularity. This sets the pattern for the entire New Covenant age.

---

## <span style="color: #2c7a7b; font-weight: bold;">6. Personal Application</span>

- When I suspect someone has done wrong, do I protect their reputation or do I expose them?
- Do I act on my first emotional reaction, or do I take time to think and pray?
- Am I willing to obey God even when it costs me my reputation or makes me look foolish in the eyes of others?
- Do I understand that the genuine work of the Holy Spirit in my life may bring misunderstanding rather than praise?

---

### <span style="color: #805ad5; font-weight: bold;">Prayer</span>

Lord Jesus, thank You for the example of Joseph. Make me like him — careful with the reputation of others, quick to pray, and ready to obey You completely. Deliver me from pride, suspicion, and impulsive reactions. Help me walk in the gentle righteousness of the New Covenant. Amen.
