> <span style="color: #374151; line-height: 1.7;">
> 1 Now after Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea in the days of Herod the king, magi from the east arrived in Jerusalem, saying,

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">1. The Magi Receive Revelation While Bible Scholars Remain Ignorant</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:1–2</span>

After Jesus was born in Bethlehem, Magi (wise men) from the East arrived in Jerusalem asking:

> <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">“Where is He who has been born King of the Jews? For we saw His star in the east and have come to worship Him.”</span>

These men lived hundreds of miles away (possibly in Persia). They had **no written Scriptures** in their own language. Yet God gave them a clear revelation through a special star.

Meanwhile, in Jerusalem — the centre of Jewish religion — there were chief priests, high priests, scribes, and Bible scholars. Gamaliel and others were running Bible schools. These men had the written Scriptures, studied them carefully, and knew them well. Yet **none of them** knew that the Messiah had been born in Bethlehem.

<span style="background-color: #fef3c7; padding: 2px 6px; border-radius: 4px; color: #92400e;">
**Very Humbling and Fearful Truth**:  
It is possible for us today, who possess the written Scriptures and study them in Bible schools, to remain completely ignorant of Jesus Christ Himself. At the same time, people who have no Bible but fear God and seek Him can receive genuine revelation.
</span>

God is no respecter of persons. In every place where people sincerely seek Him, He reveals Himself to them. This is one of the first messages of the New Testament.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">2. The First Mistake of the Wise Men – Leaning on Reason</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:1–8</span>

The star had successfully led the Magi all the way to the land of Israel. If they had continued following the star, it would have taken them straight to Bethlehem. They would never have needed to consult anyone.

But when they reached Jerusalem, they made a serious mistake. They stopped following the star and began to follow their own **reason**. They thought:  
“The King of the Jews must be born in the capital city (Jerusalem) and in the palace.”

So they went to King Herod’s palace.

<span style="background-color: #ecfdf5; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #10b981; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>The Star as a Picture of God’s Word</strong><br>
The star is a type of the prophetic Word of God. <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">2 Peter 1:19</span> tells us that the prophetic word is like a lamp shining in a dark place, which we must follow until the Morning Star (Jesus) arises in our hearts (see also <span style="color: #b91c1c;">Revelation 22:16</span>).
</span>

Until we see Jesus face to face, we must allow the Word of God to guide us.

The same mistake the Magi made is made by many believers today. We follow God’s Word up to a certain point, and then we begin to think, “Here my reason is good enough. I don’t need the guidance of the Word any longer.” The result is that we go astray.

The things of the Spirit of God are foolishness to the natural mind. The Magi could never have imagined that the King of the Jews would be born in a stable.

<span style="background-color: #fef2f2; padding: 6px 10px; border-radius: 4px; color: #991b1b;">
<strong>Key Principle (Proverbs 3:5)</strong>:  
“Trust in the Lord with all your heart and do <u>not lean on your own understanding</u>.”
</span>

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">3. Herod and the Old Religious System</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:3–8</span>

When Herod heard that a new King had been born, he was troubled, and all Jerusalem with him.

This is a picture of an old religious system that feels threatened by the revelation of new truth. People who have comfortably settled themselves on thrones — whether political, financial, or religious Christian empires — become disturbed when the true King and the New Covenant message of simplicity appear. Their position is threatened.

Herod later wanted to kill the Child. In the same way, those who sit securely on religious thrones often want to silence or “kill” those who proclaim the pure New Covenant gospel.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">4. Knowledge of Scripture Without Revelation</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:4–6</span>

Herod gathered the chief priests and scribes and asked where the Messiah was to be born. They immediately answered correctly from Scripture (Micah 5:2): “In Bethlehem of Judea.”

They had accurate academic knowledge. They could quote the exact verse. Yet they had **no revelation**. They never went to Bethlehem to seek the Child themselves.

<span style="background-color: #fef3c7; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>Tremendous Warning</strong><br>
An academic knowledge of the Scriptures, accumulated through years of Bible school study or personal study, is quite useless apart from the revelation of the Holy Spirit. We can become experts who can answer any question promptly, just like these scribes, and still remain completely blind to Jesus.
</span>

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">5. Herod’s Deception and the Need for Spiritual Wisdom</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:7–8</span>

Herod secretly called the Magi, found out the exact time the star had appeared, and sent them to Bethlehem saying, “Go and search carefully for the Child; and when you have found Him, report to me, so that I too may come and worship Him.”

This was a complete lie. Herod’s real intention was to kill the Child.

The Magi were gullible and believed him — until God later warned them in a dream.

**Practical Lesson**:  
We need to be “wise as serpents and harmless as doves.” Many people claim to be interested in the New Covenant gospel, but they are not willing to give up their private kingdoms (financial or religious). Such people cannot be true disciples of Jesus Christ.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">6. The Star Leads Them Straight to Jesus</span>
<span style="color: #6b7280;">Matthew 2:9–11</span>

When the Magi left Herod and looked up again, the star reappeared and went before them until it stopped over the exact house where the Child was.

They rejoiced with <span style="color: #b91c1c; font-weight: 600;">exceeding great joy</span>.

Notice the contrast:  
- When they leaned on their reason and went to men (Herod and the scribes), they became depressed.  
- When they returned to the guidance of the star (God’s Word), they received exceeding joy that no man can give.

They entered the house, saw the Child with Mary His mother, and fell down and **worshipped the Child** — not Mary.

Then they opened their treasures and presented gifts:
- <span style="color: #b45309; font-weight: 600;">Gold</span> – speaking of Him as **King**  
- <span style="color: #7c3aed; font-weight: 600;">Frankincense</span> – speaking of Him as **Priest**  
- <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: 600;">Myrrh</span> – speaking of Him as the **suffering Prophet and Servant**

<span style="background-color: #ecfdf5; padding: 8px 12px; border-left: 4px solid #10b981; display: block; margin: 12px 0;">
<strong>Final Principle</strong><br>
The Word of God, when followed under the guidance of the Holy Spirit, always leads us to <strong>Jesus Himself</strong> — not merely to doctrines. The Holy Spirit has come to reveal Jesus Christ in the fullness of His glory and work.
</span>

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">7. Key Spiritual Lessons</span>

- God reveals Himself to those who sincerely seek Him, even if they have no written Scriptures.
- Having the Bible and Bible knowledge is no guarantee of knowing Jesus personally.
- Never stop following the Word of God and begin leaning on your own reason.
- Religious systems and personal kingdoms feel threatened by the true King and the New Covenant message.
- Academic knowledge without the Holy Spirit’s revelation is empty and useless.
- The Word of God always leads us to Jesus Christ Himself, not just to doctrines.
- Returning to God’s Word after going astray brings exceeding great joy.

---

## <span style="color: #0f766e; font-weight: bold;">8. Personal Application</span>

- Am I still following the “star” (God’s Word by the Holy Spirit), or have I started leaning on my own reason?
- Do I have only academic knowledge of Scripture, or do I have living revelation of Jesus?
- Am I willing to leave my “Jerusalem” (comfort, reputation, religious systems) to find the real Jesus?
- When I meet Jesus, do I worship Him alone, or do I give honour to other people or things?
- Do I experience the exceeding great joy that comes from following God’s Word, or the depression that comes from leaning on men and reason?
