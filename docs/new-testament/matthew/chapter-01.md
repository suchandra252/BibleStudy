# Understanding the Genealogy of Jesus

**Matthew 1:1**

**Matthew 1:1**  
“The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham.”

---

## The Two Books of Generations

The Bible records two important “books of generations”:

- **Genesis 5:1** — “The book of the generations of Adam” (the fallen human race, marked by sin and death).
- **Matthew 1:1** — “The book of the generations of Jesus Christ” (the line of redemption and new life).

**Key Spiritual Truth**:  
Every human being is born into the family of Adam. But through repentance and faith in Jesus Christ, we can be transferred into the family of God (1 Corinthians 15:22, 45-49).

---

## Jesus’ Legal and Physical Lineage

### A. Matthew’s Genealogy (Joseph – Legal Heir)
- Traces the lineage of **Joseph**, the husband of Mary (Matthew 1:16).
- Follows the **royal line** of David through Solomon and the kings of Judah.
- Proves that Jesus is the **legal heir** to the throne of David.

### B. Luke’s Genealogy (Mary – Physical Descent)
- Traces the lineage of **Mary**.
- Follows David through his son **Nathan**.
- Proves that Jesus is physically “of the seed of David according to the flesh” (Romans 1:3).

**Summary**:  
The two genealogies together confirm that Jesus is both the **legal** and **physical** descendant of King David — fully qualified as the Messiah and King.

---

## Why Matthew Begins with Abraham

- **Matthew** wrote primarily for a **Jewish audience**, so he begins the genealogy with **Abraham**, the father of the Jewish nation.
- **Luke** wrote for the **Gentile world**, so he traces the line all the way back to **Adam**.

This shows that Jesus is the promised Saviour for both Jews and Gentiles.

# The Four Women in the Genealogy of Jesus

**A Remarkable Picture of Grace**

**Matthew 1:1-17**

One of the most surprising features in the Gospel of Matthew is the mention of **four women** in the genealogy of Jesus. In ancient Jewish genealogies, women were rarely named. Even more striking is the background of these women — each had a story marked by shame, scandal, or outsider status.

---

## The Four Women

### 1. Tamar (Matthew 1:3)
- **Story**: Judah’s daughter-in-law. After being wronged, she disguised herself as a prostitute and conceived Perez through her father-in-law (Genesis 38).
- **Significance**: A story involving deception and immorality.

### 2. Rahab (Matthew 1:5)
- **Story**: A prostitute in Jericho. She protected the Israelite spies and later married into the people of God (Joshua 2 & 6).
- **Significance**: A Canaanite woman with a morally corrupt past.

### 3. Ruth (Matthew 1:5)
- **Story**: A Moabite woman — a Gentile. The Moabites originated from incest (Genesis 19:30-37). She became the wife of Boaz.
- **Significance**: A foreigner who was normally excluded from Israel.

### 4. Bathsheba (Matthew 1:6)
- **Story**: Referred to as “the wife of Uriah.” David committed adultery with her and murdered her husband (2 Samuel 11).
- **Significance**: Part of a major royal scandal involving sin and bloodshed.

---

## Why Did God Include These Women?

Jesus deliberately chose to come through a **broken and sinful family line**.

This was not a mistake. It carries deep meaning:

- Jesus came **to save sinners**, not the self-righteous (Matthew 9:13).
- No background is too shameful for God’s grace.
- God can take what is broken and make it part of His beautiful plan.
- The Messiah identified fully with humanity’s weakness and failure.

---

## Powerful Application for Disciples

If the Son of God was willing to enter such a family line, we have no right to be proud about our own family background, caste, status, or achievements.

**Challenge**:
- Lay down all pride and superiority.
- Humbly identify with the weak and the sinner.
- Boast only in the cross of Christ (Galatians 6:14).

**Self-Examination**:
- Do I still feel superior to others because of my family or background?
- Am I willing to follow Jesus in radical humility?
