# Understanding the Genealogy of Jesus Christ

**Matthew 1:1**  
“The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham.”

---

## 1. The Two Books of Generations

The Bible records two important “books of generations”:

- **Genesis 5:1** — “The book of the generations of Adam” (fallen humanity, sin and death).
- **Matthew 1:1** — “The book of the generations of Jesus Christ” (redemption and new life).

**Key Spiritual Truth**:  
Every person is born into Adam’s family. But through repentance and faith in Jesus Christ, we can be transferred into the family of God (1 Corinthians 15:22, 45-49).

---

## 2. Jesus’ Legal and Physical Lineage

### A. Matthew’s Genealogy (Joseph – Legal Heir)
- Traces **Joseph**, husband of Mary (Matthew 1:16).
- Follows the royal line: David → Solomon → kings of Judah.
- Proves Jesus is the **legal heir** to David’s throne.

### B. Luke’s Genealogy (Mary – Physical Descent)
- Traces **Mary**’s line through David → Nathan.
- Confirms Jesus is physically “of the seed of David according to the flesh” (Romans 1:3).

**Summary**: Both genealogies together prove Jesus is the rightful Messiah — legally and physically from David’s line.

---

## 3. Why Matthew Begins with Abraham

Matthew wrote primarily for a **Jewish audience**, so he starts with Abraham.  
Luke wrote for the **Gentile world**, so he starts with Adam.

This shows Jesus is the promised Saviour for **both Jews and Gentiles**.

---

## 4. The Four Women in the Genealogy  
**A Remarkable Picture of Grace**

Matthew highlights four women — unusual in Jewish genealogies. Each had a story of shame, scandal, or outsider status:

1. **Tamar** (v.3) — Deception and immorality with her father-in-law (Genesis 38).
2. **Rahab** (v.5) — Prostitute of Jericho (Joshua 2 & 6).
3. **Ruth** (v.5) — Moabite Gentile (Genesis 19).
4. **Bathsheba** (v.6) — Adultery and murder involving David (2 Samuel 11).

---

## 5. Theological Significance

Jesus **deliberately** chose a broken and sinful family line.  

He came not to call the righteous, but **sinners to repentance** (Matthew 9:13).  
This reveals the greatness of God’s grace — no one is beyond redemption.

---

## 6. Application: True Discipleship and Humility

If the Son of God humbled Himself to enter such a line, we have no right to pride in our family, caste, or status.

**Challenge**:
- Reject all superiority over others.
- Humbly identify with the weak and sinful.
- Boast only in the cross of Christ (Galatians 6:14).

**Self-Examination**:
- Do I still feel better than others because of my background?
- Am I willing to follow Jesus in radical humility?
